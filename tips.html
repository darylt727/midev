Five Memory Boosting Foods

Better brain foods have always been a subject of intense research. Generally, the foods we think of as good for heart health, are also good for the brain; both the heart and brain require good blod flow for proper functioning. One way to improve brain health is through diet, and choosing a menu that improves blood flow to the brain can help improve memory. Consider adding the following foods to boost your brain health:

Nuts and seeds - Almonds, pecans and walnuts are a good source of Vitamin E, an antioxidant.

Add an ounce a day of: walnuts, hazelnuts, Brazil nuts, filberts, almonds, cashews, peanuts, sunflower seeds, sesame seeds, and flax seed.

Omega-3 fatty acids - Seafood, algae and fatty fish — like salmon, bluefin tuna, sardines and herring — are some of the best sources of omega-3 fatty acids.

Dark Chocolate - In addition to powerful antioxidant properties, dark chocolate contains natural stimulants, like caffeine, which enhance focus and concentration, and stimulates the production of endorphins, improving mood.

Dark-skinned fruits and vegetables. In general, dark-skinned fruits and vegetables have the highest levels of naturally occurring antioxidant levels. Antioxidants are vital in countering free radicals, which are the harmful byproducts of cellular metabolism that contribute to age-related diseases. Dark-skinned vegetables that are good sources of antioxidants include kale, spinach, broccoli, and eggplant. Fruits with high antioxidant levels include prunes, raisins, blueberries, blackberries, strawberries, raspberries, plums, oranges, red grapes and cherries.

Whole grains - Whole grains, such as oatmeal, whole-grain breads, and brown rice can reduce the risk for heart disease, and improve the blood flow required for good brain function.

Foods to Avoid

Recent research has shed more light on what to avoid for better brain health. Here are five foods that should be avoided:

Fried Foods - Your brain depends on good blood flow as much as your heart does. High levels of saturated fats and cholesterol and can cause clots in the blood vessels of the brain. High intakes of the saturated fats and cholesterol contained in high-fat, fried foods clogs the arteries and decreases blood flow. If grilling or baking isn't an option, try mono- and polyunsaturated fats.

Alcohol and nicotine - Nicotine impedes the blood flow to brain while Alcohol produces memory loos and blackouts. Excessive intake of nicotine and alcohol is linked with memory loss and reduction in ability to perform brain function.

Junk Food - High-fat snacks can cause fatigue and zap alertness. Unhealthy snacks - candy, cookies, chips - in above-moderate consumption cause more cognitive errors from lapses in concentration and attention.

Diacetyl (Butter Flavoring) - Research suggests that that bag of innocent-looking popcorn containing diacetyl - an additive capable of crossing the blood-brain barrier. This defense mechanism keeps toxins at bay. 

Diacetyl causes beta-amyloid clumping, a risk associated with Alzheimer's. Read the label, and if you find 'artificial butter flavor', you might want to consider avoiding it in favor of stove-popped popcorn.
